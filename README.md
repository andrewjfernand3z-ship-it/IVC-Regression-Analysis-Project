Performed a regression analysis project in a Statistics & Probability 
course using Microsoft Excel to evaluate relationships between behavioral outcomes and demographic, 
academic, and workload-related variables. Analyzed data involving STEM major status, 
workload outside of school, race, household size, and academic unit load.

Applied hypothesis testing and p-value analysis to identify statistically significant 
relationships within the dataset. Findings indicated that external workload demonstrated 
the strongest correlation with shower frequency, while variables such as academic major and race showed limited statistical significance. 
Presented conclusions through organized data interpretation and quantitative analysis.

Our analysis found no statistically significant evidence that 
Academic workload or demographics correlate to personal hygiene. However, regression results 
indicated that external workload demonstrated the strongest relationship with shower frequency, suggesting that
work-related responsibilities may have a greater influence on personal habits than academic major or demographic factors. 
While some demographic variables showed minor correlations within the dataset, they were not as statistically significant as workload.
